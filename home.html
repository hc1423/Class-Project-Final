While rental car companies and taxis may seem like very different services, at their core, both exist to fulfill the same basic need: providing convenient, short-term access to a vehicle for transportation. In many ways, rental car businesses are the modern, self-driven equivalent of taxis. Where traditional taxis offer on-demand rides with a driver, rental car companies empower customers to be their own driver, giving them control over their schedule, route, and pace. With app-based booking, keyless entry, and flexible pickup/drop-off locations, modern rental services mirror the convenience of hailing a cab—except the user gets the privacy and freedom of driving themselves. Much like how ride-share services blurred the lines between private car use and taxi service, rental car companies are increasingly becoming part of the same ecosystem—especially with hourly rentals, app integrations, and vehicles placed throughout cities in ready-to-use “car hubs.” This transforms the rental model into a self-service taxi experience, minus the meter and the driver. In essence, a rental car today is a taxi you control.
